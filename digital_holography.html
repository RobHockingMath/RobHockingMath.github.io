The Beginning



Until the summer of 2023 I - like most people - was barely aware of the existence of holography as a field. To the extent that I thought of it at all, I thought of the low quality holograms from the 80's and 90's I'd seen as a kid (perhaps they weren't even holograms, they could have been low quality lenticular sheets).

This all changed in the spring of 2023, when my friend Sydney Koke contacted me about meeting up in Taiwan, where I live. Due to COVID, we hadn't seen each other in several years, and as she happened to be going to Korea that July for a conference and so would be in the area, it seemed like a low cost way of getting a visit in (normally we live on different continents). However, with Sydney arriving in late June and with Taiwan at that time of year being hot enough to kill, I struggled to think of what we could do. Fortunately, Sydney had developed a new passion in the years since we had last seen each other - holography. She decided that since it was too hot to go outside, we would simply spend the week making analog holograms in my apartment.

Sydney gave me a shopping list of items to procure - a low cost green laser, photoemulsion film, black velvet, plenty of small picture frames, etc. Since I don't have a darkroom in my apartment, we simply worked at night - staying up until 4AM every day that she was here, making very basic analog holograms. Sydney's enthuasism was infectious, and so I ended up deciding to go to Korea with her and attend the conference (ISDH 2023) as well. The quality of the holograms I saw there astonished me, and when I learnt that one could use digital holography to make holograms of objects that exist only within the confines of your computer, I quickly realized that this could be the ideal artistic medium for mathematical objects not conducive to 3D printing. I was hooked.
